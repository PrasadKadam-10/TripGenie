Cherrapunji, also known as Sohra, is a famous hill destination in Meghalaya known for its lush green landscapes, heavy rainfall, waterfalls, and unique living root bridges. Located in the East Khasi Hills, it is one of the wettest places on Earth and offers breathtaking views of mist-covered valleys, limestone caves, and dramatic cliffs. The region is rich in natural beauty and tribal culture, making it one of Indiaâ€™s most unique travel destinations.

One of the biggest attractions of Cherrapunji is the Double Decker Living Root Bridge, a natural architectural marvel created by guiding tree roots over decades. The destination also features spectacular waterfalls like Nohkalikai Falls, Seven Sisters Falls, and Dainthlen Falls, which are among the tallest and most scenic waterfalls in India. The Mawsmai Caves offer thrilling cave exploration experiences with natural rock formations and underground passages.

Tourists can enjoy trekking, waterfall exploration, cave visits, cultural interactions with Khasi tribes, and panoramic valley viewpoints. The best time to visit Cherrapunji is from October to May for clear skies and accessible trekking routes. By Road: Well connected from Shillong (approx. 54 km). By Rail: Nearest railway station is Guwahati (approx. 150 km). By Air: Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport, Guwahati. Travelers should carry rain protection, trekking shoes, and lightweight clothing due to frequent rainfall. Cherrapunji is ideal for adventure lovers, nature explorers, and eco-tourism experiences in Northeast India.
