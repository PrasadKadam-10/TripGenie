Shimla, the capital of Himachal Pradesh, is one of India’s most famous hill stations known for its colonial architecture, cool climate, and scenic Himalayan landscapes. Located at an altitude of about 2,200 meters, Shimla was once the summer capital of British India and still retains its old-world charm through heritage buildings, churches, and promenades. Surrounded by pine forests and rolling hills, it is a perfect mountain getaway for families and nature lovers.

Key attractions include The Ridge, Mall Road, Jakhoo Temple, Kufri, and Christ Church, which reflect the historical and cultural legacy of the region. Visitors can enjoy toy train rides on the UNESCO-listed Kalka–Shimla Railway, trekking through forest trails, horse riding in Kufri, and snow activities during winter. The pleasant climate and scenic viewpoints provide a refreshing escape from city life.

The best time to visit Shimla is from March to June for pleasant weather and December to February for snowfall experiences. By Air: Jubbarhatti Airport (approx. 23 km). By Rail: Kalka Railway Station connected via toy train. By Road: Well connected from Chandigarh and Delhi via highways. Travelers should carry warm clothes in winter and book accommodations early during peak tourist seasons. Shimla is ideal for honeymoon trips, family vacations, and mountain tourism.
